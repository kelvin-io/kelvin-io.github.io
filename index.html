Sign international agreements Only through international cooperation can the problem of global warming be eased. At the Paris Climate Conference held in 2015, 195 countries accepted the agreement of legally-binding emission cuts. This agreement will enter into force in 2020 after the expiry of the Kyoto Protocol.

F2 Geog Project Research

China VI refers to a set of emission standards implemented in China to regulate the pollution caused by vehicles. These standards aim to reduce the amount of harmful pollutants released into the air by vehicles, such as nitrogen oxides (NOx) and particulate matter (PM). China VI is the strictest emission standard in China to date, building upon the previous China V standard. It requires vehicles to have advanced technologies and control systems to minimize their environmental impact. By implementing China VI, the Chinese government strives to improve air quality, protect public health, and promote sustainable transportation.
Euro VI is a set of emission standards that define the maximum amount of pollutants that vehicles can release into the air. It applies to cars, trucks, and buses sold in Europe. Euro VI is the most stringent and advanced emission standard to date, aiming to reduce harmful substances like nitrogen oxides (NOx) and particulate matter (PM) from vehicle exhaust. This helps improve air quality and protects our health by reducing pollution from vehicles on the road.
